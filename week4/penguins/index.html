Penguins



Penguins are flightless seabirds known for their distinctive black and white plumage and upright, waddling gait. Belonging to the family Spheniscidae, they are highly adapted for life in the water, with streamlined bodies and strong flippers that enable them to swim efficiently. Most species are found in the Southern Hemisphere, particularly in Antarctica, although some live in temperate or even tropical regions such as South Africa, New Zealand, and the Gal√°pagos Islands.
